Implementation Based Paper: Parallel and Distributed Deep Learning Techniques for Real-Time Object Detection

Object detection is a critical task in computer vision, enabling systems to identify and localize objects within images. It plays a key role in applications such as autonomous vehicles, surveillance systems, robotics, and smart city infrastructure. However, training modern object detection models like YOLOv5 and Faster R-CNN is computationally expensive due to their deep architectures and large-scale datasets. This project aims to investigate and evaluate the efficiency of parallel and distributed training methods to accelerate the training process of object detection models without sacrificing performance.

Key Areas of Investigation

  • Algorithmic Techniques: Investigating scheduling algorithms such as list scheduling, priority-based heuristics, critical path methods, and task graph partitioning.
  • High-Performance Computing (HPC) Considerations: Exploring parallel computing strategies for GPUs, TPUs, and multi-core architectures, along with memory-aware optimizations.
  • ML-Based Scheduling Approaches: Examining reinforcement learning and neural network-driven auto-scheduling techniques in frameworks like TensorFlow XLA, Apache TVM, and PyTorch TorchScript.

This survey will provide a comparative analysis of scheduling strategies, highlight existing challenges, and identify future research directions for improving scheduling efficiency in AI/ML workflows.
